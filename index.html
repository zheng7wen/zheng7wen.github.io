    Theoretical Model for a Highly Sensitive Near Infrared Biosensor Based on Bloch Surface Wave with Dirac Semimetal
    Qiwen Zheng, Yamei Liu, Wenguang Lu, Xiaoyu Dai, Haishan Tian, Leyong Jiang
    Biosensors 2021, 11
    Institute: Hunan Normal University, National University of Defense Technology, Hunan University
    [Paperlink], [Code]
    Area: Biosensor, Bloch surface wave, Dirac semimetal, Photonic crystal.

    We propose a highly sensitive near-infrared biosensor theoretical model based on BSW (Bloch surface wave). By embedding BDS (bulk Dirac semimetal) in the defect layer, the sensitivity of the whole structure is enhanced. This also provides dynamically adjustable sensing characteristics.

    High Sensitivity Terahertz Biosensor Based on Mode Coupling of a Graphene/Bragg Reflector Hybrid Structure
    Yamei Liu, Qiwen Zheng, Hongxia Yuan, Shenping Wang, Keqiang Yin, Xiaoyu Dai, Xiao Zou, Leyong Jiang.
    Biosensors 2021, 11
    Institute: Hunan Normal University, Hunan University
    [Paperlink], [Code]
    Area: Terahertz biosensor, Tamm plasmons, Mode coupling, Graphene.

    We proposed a high-sensitivity terahertz (THz) biosensor by using a graphene/Bragg reflector hybrid structure.Through specific parameter settings, the composite structure can achieve both a liquid biosensor and a gas biosensor. Besides，the maximum sensitivity of > 1000 °/RIU can be obtained.

    Enhanced and Controllable Reflected Group Delay Based on Tamm Surface Plasmons with Dirac Semimetals
    Qiwen Zheng, Wenguang Lu, Shenping Wang, Xinmin Zhao, Leyong Jiang
    is being published on, Chinese Physics B
    Institute: Hunan Normal University, National University of Defense Technology
    [Paperlink], [Code]
    Area: Group delay, Tamm plasmons, Dirac semimetals, Enhancement.

    By studying the regulation of the reflected group delay of the middle infrared band vertical incident pulse when the OTS is excited in the BDS-1D PC structure，we found the regulation of group delay mainly depends on the conductivity characteristics of the BDS at the excitation frequency of the OTS (such as the conductivity change of the BDS). At the same time, we also found that the group delay can not only be regulated by the external voltage which leads to the change of the Fermi level, but can also obtain a huge group delay by changing the parameters of the composite structure.
